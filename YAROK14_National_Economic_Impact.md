## 1. The National Opportunity

### India's Structural Energy Vulnerability

India faces a compounding crisis in domestic energy: imported LPG dependence, underperforming biogas infrastructure, and a vast untapped organic waste stream.

| National Metric | Data |
|---|---|
| LPG consumption (2024–25) | 33.15 MMT |
| LPG imported share | ~60% |
| Import route vulnerability | ~90% through Strait of Hormuz |
| Annual LPG subsidy burden | ₹30,000+ crore |
| Households dependent on LPG | ~300 million |
| Biogas plants in India | 4.9 million |
| SATAT CBG plants operational (target: 5,000) | ~130+ |
| Total untreated organic waste (annual) | ~700 million tonnes |
| Current utilisation rate of organic waste | <2% |

The 2025–26 West Asia conflict exposed this vulnerability acutely, forcing the Government of India to invoke emergency LPG measures — a scenario that will repeat unless structural domestic alternatives are built.

### The Biogas Failure Pattern

A January 2025 TERI study of 11 SATAT plants found:

| Problem | Plants Affected |
|---|---|
| No monitoring system | 8 of 11 (73%) |
| Silent production loss (up to 30%) | 8 of 11 (73%) |
| Insufficient operator knowledge | All 11 |
| pH crash events (undetected) | Multiple |
| Temperature management failures | Multiple |

The root cause is not hardware failure — it is the **complete absence of digital intelligence**. YAROK14 gives every plant a brain.

### YAROK14's Core Performance Advantage

The system's 3-stage optimised anaerobic digestion process delivers:

| Metric | Conventional | YAROK14 Optimised | Improvement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total cycle time | 46–112 days | ~6 days | Up to 18x faster |
| Daily gas output (2 m³ digester) | 0.6–1.0 m³/day | 1.5–2.5 m³/day | ~2.5x higher |
| Methane content | 55–65% | 60–70% | Improved |
| Monitoring | None | Real-time SCADA, 24/7 | Full intelligence |
| Energy overhead | Uncontrolled | Capped at ≤10% of prior day output | Financially disciplined |

India has made CBG blending in CNG/PNG legally mandatory:

| FY 2025–26 | 1% |

| Impact | Value |

India's mandatory CBG blending obligation (1%–5% in CNG/PNG) means more local biomethane displaces imported LNG at the margin. Each productive YAROK14 plant contributes to:

- Lower spot-market LNG exposure
- More resilient city gas supply
- Lower infrastructure stress in peak demand periods
- Support for India's SATAT target of 5,000 operational CBG plants

**Import-related savings only (LPG + NG/LNG side), at 1 lakh sites:**
